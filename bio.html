Derek DuPont


Derek DuPont is a senior at Tufts University majoring in American Chemical Society - certified chemistry with a minor in economics. His major coursework focuses on biological and analytical chemistry. This is reflected in Derek's research on optimizing lateral flow biosensor assays for maternal ancestry genotyping and for the detection of genetically modified soy products. Derek's current work is on the development of an educational platform for high school and college undergraduates utilizing next generation DNA sequencing technology.

In addition to his research, Derek is active in chemistry outreach, both through the green chemistry organization Beyond Benign and through his school's own department, where he is president of the local student ACS chapter.

Outside of chemistry, Derek is a student DJ at WMFO, where he hosts a weekly show featuring new music of varying genres. He also likes learning about programming, having taken several computer science courses at Tufts.
